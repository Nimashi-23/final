Coral Reef Ecosystems

Introduction

Coral reefs are among the most diverse and productive ecosystems on the planet. They are home to thousands of marine creatures and play a crucial role in maintaining marine life.

Save Our Ocean

Importance of Coral Reefs

Important ecosystem services like coastal protection, marine life habitat, and a source of food and income for millions of people worldwide are all made possible by coral reefs.

  • Storm and erosion protection for the coast
  • A variety of marine species' habitat
  • Support for local economies through fishing and tourism

Threats to Coral Reefs

Coral reefs are under threat from human activities and climate change. These threats include:

  • Pollution (e.g., plastic, oil spills)
  • Climate change (leading to coral bleaching)
  • Overfishing and destructive fishing practices
  • Coastal development

Conservation Efforts

Efforts are being made globally to protect and restore coral reefs. These efforts include:

  • Marine protected areas (MPAs)
  • Reef restoration projects
  • Promoting sustainable fishing practices
  • Reducing pollution and carbon emissions
